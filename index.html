7-Day Bengali Diet Plan for Weight Loss

⚠️ CAUTION: Consult a doctor before starting this plan, especially with late-night meals, to ensure it suits your health needs.

Designed for a 25-year-old woman, 150 cm, 65 kg (BMI 28.9), this balanced plan targets ~1,200 calories daily to reduce belly fat and bloating in one week. It incorporates your preferred Bengali foods—sattu drink, coffee, rice, dal, your mother’s sabji, papad, fruits, momo, roti, paneer bhurji—avoiding fish and chicken. Expect ~0.5 kg weight loss and a flatter belly from reduced bloating.

Guidelines

  • Calories: ~1,200/day for a safe weight loss deficit.
  • Hydration: 8-10 glasses water/day to reduce bloating.
  • Exercise: 30 min brisk walking daily (~150-200 kcal burned).
  • Sleep: 7-8 hours/night to support metabolism (avoid staying up past midnight to minimize late-night hunger).
  • Portions: Use smaller plates to control intake, especially for late meals.
  • Beverages: Choose low-calorie options like green tea or lemon water when out.

Sample Recipes

Sattu Drink

2 tbsp sattu powder, 1 glass water, pinch of salt, 1 tsp lemon juice. Mix sattu with water, add salt and lemon, stir well. ~100 kcal.

Moong Dal

50g moong dal, 1 tsp mustard oil, turmeric, cumin, 1 tomato. Cook dal with spices and tomato until soft. ~150 kcal.

Mother’s Sabji (Example: Pumpkin-Spinach)

100g mixed veggies (pumpkin, spinach), 1 tsp mustard oil, turmeric, cumin. Sauté spices, add veggies, cook with minimal water. ~100 kcal.

Paneer Bhurji

100g paneer, 50g veggies (onion, tomato), 1 tsp mustard oil, spices (turmeric, cumin). Sauté veggies and spices, add crumbled paneer, cook until blended. ~250 kcal.
