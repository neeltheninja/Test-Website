7-Day Balanced Bengali Diet Plan

This plan is crafted for a 25-year-old woman, 150 cm tall, 65 kg, aiming to reduce belly fat and bloating in one week. It’s tailored to a Bengali diet, avoiding fish and chicken, with ~1,200 calories daily—a safe deficit from your ~1,700-calorie TDEE. Expect slight weight loss (~0.5 kg) and a flatter belly from reduced bloating.

Guidelines

  • Hydration: Drink 8-10 glasses of water daily to flush sodium and reduce bloating.
  • Avoid: Fried foods, sweets, high-sodium dishes.
  • Exercise: 30 min brisk walking daily (~150-200 kcal burned).
  • Sleep: 7-8 hours nightly for metabolism support.
  • Portions: Use smaller plates to control intake.

Sample Recipes

Chirer Pulao

100g flattened rice, 50g veggies (peas, carrots), 1 tsp mustard oil, turmeric, cumin. Rinse rice, sauté spices and veggies, add rice, cook until soft. ~200 kcal.

Moong Dal

50g moong dal, 1 tsp mustard oil, turmeric, cumin, 1 tomato. Cook dal with spices and tomato. ~200 kcal.

Shukto

100g mixed veggies (bitter gourd, pumpkin), 1 tsp mustard oil, panch phoron, 50 ml milk. Sauté spices, add veggies, cook with milk. ~150 kcal.

Notes

This 1,200-calorie plan is safe for one week but consult a doctor for longer use. Bloating reduction (via yogurt, low-sodium foods) may make your belly appear flatter faster than fat loss alone. Results vary—monitor how you feel and adjust if needed.
